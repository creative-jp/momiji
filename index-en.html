A Tranquil Hideaway Nestled in the Pristine Forests of the Hakuba Valley

The Lodge

Momiji is a cosy 7 guest-room family run lodge in the North-East of the Hakuba Valley. The lodge adjoins and offers easy ski-in / ski-out access to both Cortina and Norikura resorts during the Winter season and makes an ideal base for hiking, mountain biking and exploring in the Green season.

In addition to offering accommodation to individual guests, Momiji is also available for exclusive booking by groups - ideal for yoga, company meetings, events and parties. Please enquire for group rates.

Bar & Restaurant

Momiji’s bar and restaurant features high ceilings showcasing timber beams reclaimed from a 150 year old sake brewery. Pine flooring in the common areas benefit from underfloor heating and the sofas in front of the real log fire proves a popular spot for guests to relax.

A fully stocked bar offers a selection of quality new-world wines, sake, beverages and local ales and the restaurant offers Japanese meals prepared by the owner from fresh local produce. Vegetarian and gluten-free requests are welcomed.

Whether indulging in a delicious home-cooked fare, an ale or sake, enjoying a book from the extensive library, or discussing the day’s activities with new friends or old by the log fire, the lodge offers a perfect homely atmosphere where guests can relax after a day on the slopes.

Guest Services

Momiji guests enjoy a complimentary pick-up and drop-off to Hakuba Valley train and bus terminals upon arrival and departure. Additionally, an evening shuttle is available to central Hakuba for ATM, supermarket and restaurants drop-offs (we are happy to recommend and book restaurants for you either prior to your travel or after arrival).

Location

Momiji is positioned in pristine forest, ski-in & ski-out to the Cortina and Norikura resorts, at the north-east end of the Hakuba Valley in Nagano prefecture, Japan. There are multiple ways to travel from Narita and Haneda Airports to Momiji, including:

Shinkansen

Shinkansen (the bullet train) to Nagano Station and then bus to Hakuba.

Riding the bullet train is like a holiday in itself - a must try! However…if this is your first time in Japan and you’ve just arrived after a long flight with lots of luggage, it might be a bit taxing, so consider a layover in Tokyo, or try the Nagano Snow Shuttle, or a private charter.

If you’re up for the fun of the Bullet train, you’ll need the timetable in English, our detailed guide which will walk you through a trip from Haneda or Narita to Momiji and this bus timetable to cross reference your train with.

Taxi from Nagano Station

If you prefer to take a taxi from Nagano Station, we can arrange a pickup for you with a regular taxi (maximum 4 people - limited luggage space - approx ¥10,040 JPY), or a jumbo taxi (maximum 7 people - approx ¥13,410 JPY). Contact us early to reserve.

The Nagano Snow Shuttle

Daily Airport to and from Ski-resort and inter-resort buses from December 21st through February 28th. They will drop you close to Momiji when you select the “Otari Accommodation Drop-Off” option. Check prices and reserve online.

Private Charter

By far the most stress-free option for getting from the airport or Tokyo. Cost-effective for larger groups. Contact us for details and prices. Early booking recommended.

Useful information

Generally, there is no need to reserve tickets for the bullet train or busses except around New Year and some Japanese public holidays. Unfortunately, you cannot reserve the bullet train from outside Japan, so it is only practicable to reserve once you are in Japan and have enough time to get to a Japan Railways ticket office before heading up to Hakuba.
